Privacy Policy

Effective 4 June 2026 · Econstellar research platform & LinkedIn application

Econstellar is an open, non-commercial research platform maintained by Avishek Bhandari at the School of Humanities, Social Sciences and Management, Indian Institute of Technology Bhubaneswar. This policy explains what data the platform and its associated LinkedIn application access, how that data is used, and how to contact us. It covers the public website (avishekb9.github.io/econstellar), the compute API, and the Econstellar LinkedIn application used to publish the project's own research updates.

1. What we collect

Public website and workbench. The website requires no account and no sign-in. We do not collect names, email addresses, or other personal information from visitors, and we do not use advertising or cross-site tracking. The only usage signal recorded is an aggregate, non-identifying count of page views and feature uses (for example, how many times a method was run), with no IP address, no cookie, and no personal identifier stored. Analyses you run on the public engine are processed transiently to return a result and are not linked to you.

LinkedIn application. The Econstellar LinkedIn application is operated solely by the project author to publish the project's own research announcements to the author's own LinkedIn account. Through LinkedIn's OAuth 2.0 authorisation it accesses only: the authorising user's basic profile (name and LinkedIn member identifier) and permission to create posts on that user's behalf (the w_member_social scope). It does not read connections, messages, feeds, or any other LinkedIn members' data.

2. How we use it

  • To operate the research platform and return the results you request.
  • To publish research updates that the operator authors, to the operator's own LinkedIn account.
  • Aggregate, non-identifying usage counts help us understand which features are useful and guide development.

We do not build profiles of individuals, and we do not use any LinkedIn data for advertising or for any purpose other than publishing the operator's own posts.

3. Storage and security

The LinkedIn access token obtained during authorisation is stored securely on the operator's own server and is used only to publish content the operator initiates. It is never sold, shared, or transferred to third parties. The aggregate usage counts described above contain no personal data.

4. Sharing and disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or share personal data with third parties. Data accessed through the LinkedIn API is used strictly to provide the posting function described above, consistent with the LinkedIn API Terms of Use and the LinkedIn Platform Guidelines.

5. Third-party services

The website is hosted on GitHub Pages. Computation and AI-assisted features use Google Cloud services (Vertex AI / Gemini). Publishing uses the LinkedIn API. Each of these providers processes requests under its own privacy policy.

6. Your choices and rights

You may revoke the Econstellar application's access to your LinkedIn account at any time from your LinkedIn account settings (Data privacy → Permitted services). To ask a question, or to request deletion of any data associated with you, contact us at the address below and we will respond promptly.

7. Children

Econstellar is a scholarly tool and is not directed to children under the age of 16, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

8. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the revised version will be posted on this page with a new effective date.
